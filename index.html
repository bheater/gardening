Garden Design

There are three fundamental factors to consider in any garden:

  1. Location
  2. Climate
  3. Light
  4. Style
  5. Space

1. Location

Where will your garden be located?

A garden is traditionally located in the frontyard, backyard, patio, or deck. Indoor plants have also been gaining popularity. My garden resides on a covered deck and revieves ample indirect sunlight all day. Most gardens will be outside, but climate and shade will vary, depending on your geographic location and orientation to the sun.

2. Climate

Climate is the most important factor to ensure the longevity of your plants and the success of your garden. Choose plants known to thrive within your USDA Hardiness Zone.

The USDA Hardiness Zone Map divides North America into climate zones to better inform which types of plants will grow well in your area.

Check out the USDA Hardiness Zone Finder to input your zip code and find the Hardiness Zone for your garden. Select plants categorized within your climate zone.

3. Light

How many hours of direct sunlight is your garden is exposed to?

Sunlight is the next, most important aspect of properly planning plants for your garden.

    Exposure Definition
    Full sun At least 6 hours of direct sunlight
    Partial sun or partial shade 3-6 hours of direct sunlight
    Shade Less than 3 hours of direct sunlight with the bulk of the sun exposure occurring during either early morning, late afternoon, or dappled sunlight (filtered) throughout the day.

4. Style

How do you want your garden to look and feel?

The key to determine the right style of a garden is to determine how the garden should make you feel. If I want to feel relaxed, I might design the garden with lush, exotic, tropical trees and pinapple to remind me of vacations to Hawaii and the Bahamas. Succulents and cacti from Arizona remind me of friends and the beauty of the desert. Incorporating a myriad of plants in a uniform style (i.e. terra cotta pot) can be create an aestheically pleasing harmony that evokes wonderful memories.

What aesthetic or theme will your garden embue?

  • Modern and minimalist
  • Regal, stately, and traditional
  • Rustic and cottage-esque

5. Space

How large will your garden be?

The location and size of your garden will determine if any plant stands or planters will be neccessary. For instance, plant stands and planters are especially helpful when creating a garden on a patio or deck. The style you determined in the previous section can help inform which designs to choose.
